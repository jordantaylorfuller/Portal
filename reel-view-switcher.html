Five ways to switch views.

Replacing the three text tabs with something that reads as part of the editorial fabric — film, transport, viewfinder. Each option is live; click around to feel it.

Diagram glyphs
01 / 05

The icon is the layout. Three hairline diagrams in a hairline-bordered segmented control — a literal map of what you'll see. Closest to the existing tab control; lowest-risk swap.

Film strip
02 / 05

Three frames between sprocket holes — borrows from a 35mm contact strip. Emphasises the studio's craft. The active frame is keyed with the red accent like a circle take.

Most expressive · slightly larger footprint List
Sliding underline
03 / 05

Pure typography: three monospace labels, a single accent hairline that slides between them. Feels like an editorial timeline marker. Lightest visual weight; relies on words, not glyphs.

Closest to the current bar · no icons List
Aperture dial
04 / 05

A camera-mode dial paired with a tight glyph stack. The needle rotates to the chosen mode; keys (L/C/S) double as a keyboard hint. Most "film studio" of the bunch.

Distinctive · needs more space List
Transport track
05 / 05

A three-stop scrubber with a red playhead — directly echoes the video transport bar that already lives on the reel page. The view becomes a position on a timeline; clicking a stop seeks to it.
